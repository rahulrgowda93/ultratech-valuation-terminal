THE INVESTMENT THESIS · IN PLAIN TERMS
◎
THE BULL CASE
  • India's per-capita cement consumption at 260 kg vs global average 570 kg — structural demand gap doesn't close at 5%
  • India Cements integration delivers ₹800 Cr guided synergies by FY27 — already tracking ahead on EBITDA/tonne recovery (₹140 YoY improvement)
  • Utilisation ramp from 60% → 80% creates above-fundamental growth without proportionate capex
  • Net Debt/EBITDA collapses from 1.08× to <0.3× by FY28 — enables buybacks and dividend step-up
  • ROIC–WACC spread expected to widen to 4–5% as acquired assets earn through their potential
⚠
THE BEAR CASE
  • 88.2% of EV is terminal value — requires India's construction cycle to remain durable for decades
  • South India pricing rationalisation may take longer than modelled — Adani-backed Ambuja accelerating market share push
  • Cables & wires venture risks misallocating ₹5,000 Cr at sub-WACC returns — high-growth bet inside a Mature Growth company
  • Sustained Indian rate rise + ROIC–WACC spread compression + failed integration = bear scenario ₹6,800–₹7,200
  • Sales-to-Capital ratio of 1.05× means growth is never "free" — a permanent drag on near-term FCF
→
THE STRATEGIC MANDATE
  • Deepen South India position through India Cements — accelerate Tier 2/3 distribution, use acquired network as zero-capex upside
  • Hold cables & wires to a verifiable 15%+ ROIC threshold within 4 years before scaling
  • Prioritise deleveraging to 0.6× Net Debt/EBITDA by FY27 before returning excess capital aggressively
  • Accelerate renewable energy to 60% of power mix by FY28 — every 1% energy/revenue reduction = ₹760 Mn EBIT
  • Do not acquire further until India Cements is fully absorbed. Do not mistake Mature Growth for High Growth.
